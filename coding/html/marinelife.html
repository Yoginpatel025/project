Marine Life

Discover the incredible diversity of life beneath the waves.

Marine ecosystems are complex and interconnected, with each species contributing to the functioning of the ecosystem as a whole.

The Ocean

The ocean covers more than 70% of the Earth's surface, making it the largest habitat on the planet.

It plays a crucial role in regulating the climate by absorbing heat and carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

Oceans are home to a vast array of ecosystems, from coral reefs to deep-sea trenches, supporting diverse marine life.

They provide vital resources such as food, medicine, and minerals, sustaining billions of livelihoods worldwide.

Human activities like overfishing, pollution, and climate change threaten the health of our oceans and marine biodiversity.

Notable Species

Whales

Whales are large marine mammals known for their intelligence and complex behaviors.

Sharks

Sharks are a group of elasmobranch fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton and a streamlined body.

Coral

Corals are marine invertebrates that typically live in compact colonies of many identical individual polyps.

Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines and are characterized by a bony or cartilaginous shell.

Dolphins

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals known for their agility and playful behavior.

Octopus

Octopuses are cephalopods known for their intelligence, ability to camouflage, and unique locomotion.

Seahorses

Seahorses are small marine fish known for their horse-like heads and unique mode of reproduction.

Jellyfish

Jellyfish are gelatinous zooplankton that have a bell-shaped body and tentacles, some of which can deliver a painful sting.

Extinct Sea Animals

Megalodon

The Megalodon was one of the largest and most powerful predators in vertebrate history, measuring up to 60 feet.

Dunkleosteus

Dunkleosteus was a prehistoric fish known for its armored head and powerful jaws.

Ichthyosaur

Ichthyosaurs were large marine reptiles that resembled modern dolphins.

Plesiosaur

Plesiosaurs were marine reptiles known for their long necks and small heads.

Mosasaur

Mosasaurs were large marine reptiles that dominated the oceans during the late Cretaceous period.

Overview

Marine life, or sea life or ocean life, is the plants, animals, and other organisms that live in the saltwater of the sea or ocean, or the brackish water of coastal estuaries. At a fundamental level, marine life helps determine the very nature of our planet. Marine organisms produce oxygen and sequester carbon. Shorelines are in part shaped and protected by marine life, and some marine organisms even help create new land.
