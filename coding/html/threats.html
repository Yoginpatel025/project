Threats to Marine Life

Explore the various challenges faced by marine ecosystems and species.

Climate Change

Climate change impacts marine life through ocean warming, acidification, and altered habitats.

Overfishing

Overfishing depletes fish stocks, disrupts ecosystems, and threatens species with extinction.

Pollution

Marine pollution from plastic debris, chemicals, and oil spills harms marine organisms and ecosystems.

Habitat Destruction

Destruction of coastal habitats like coral reefs and mangroves reduces biodiversity and disrupts marine life.

Invasive Species

Invasive species disrupt native ecosystems and compete with local species for resources.

Ocean Acidification

Increased CO2 levels in the atmosphere lead to ocean acidification, affecting marine organisms with calcium carbonate shells.

Illegal, Unreported, and Unregulated (IUU) Fishing

IUU fishing practices undermine fisheries management efforts, threatening marine biodiversity and food security.

Sea Level Rise

Rising sea levels due to climate change can flood coastal habitats and disrupt marine ecosystems.

Plastic Entanglement

Marine animals can become entangled in plastic debris, leading to injury, suffocation, and death.

Chemical Pollution

Toxic chemicals from agricultural runoff, industrial waste, and personal care products contaminate marine environments, affecting marine life.

Loss of Biodiversity

The loss of marine biodiversity due to human activities reduces ecosystem resilience and affects ecological balance.
